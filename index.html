ABOUT ME

Howdy, I’m Jerray! [pronounced ‘JERR-ee’ or 杰瑞 | Pronouns: he/him].

I’m a Dallas-based electrical engineer and maker that focuses on robotics, wood art, and much more! This website serves as my personal portfolio/resume site so feel free to stop by from time to time to check up on what I’m doing. I was born in Vancover, Washington, but I’ve lived the majority of my life in DFW. In high school, I participated in the FIRST Robotics Competition which gave me a love for robots and engineering and shaped my future aspirations. I attended Case Western Reserve University (CWRU) in Cleveland for college and I spent a good portion of my time making stuff and working at think[box], CWRU’s makerspace. My experience at think[box] (both as a user and as an employee) gave me a love for making. I currently work for an educational robotics company in DFW and I typically spend my free time mentoring a FRC team (2714 BBQ), going on adventures with friends, or finding great new food.

FRC HISTORY

FRC 2714 BBQ

The 2019 season was my first year back in the FRC scene after a four year hiatus. This was also my first time as a full-time mentor and I must say this experience was an exciting adventure with many lessons learned.

2019 - Brisket


FRC 2848 The All-Sparks

The All-Sparks was my FRC team in high school. I spent all four years of high school participating in FRC and, looking back, the experience was probably one of the most significant influences in my life as a teenager. Throughout my time on the team, I held the position of programming team captain, design team captain, pitboss, and vice-president. In the 2014 season, I got to experience winning the World Championship in the pits on Curie and on Einstein. My time on the All-Sparks gave me a love for robots and engineering and I'll never forget the memories I made along the journey.
