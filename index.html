ABOUT ME

Howdy, I’m Jerray! [pronounced ‘JERR-ee’ or 杰瑞 | Pronouns: he/him].

I'm an electrical engineer and maker based in Dallas, Texas. I mainly focus on robotics and woodworking, but the stuff I work can range anywhere from robotics to digital fashion to even cardboard furniture! This website serves as my personal portfolio/resume site so feel free to stop by from time to time to check up on what I’m doing. In regards to my background, I was born in Vancover, Washington, but I’ve lived the majority of my life in DFW. In high school, I participated in the FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC) on team 2848 The All-Sparks. FRC gave me a love for robots and engineering and was a major influence on my future aspirations. I attended Case Western Reserve University (CWRU) in Cleveland to study electrical engineering and I spent a good portion of my time making stuff and working at CWRU's makerspace, think[box]. My time at think[box] was a unique experience, as I not only collaborated with engineering and business students from CWRU but also with art students from the Cleveland Institute of Art. Many of the projects featured in my portfolio are the result of opportunities from this intersection of engineering and art at think[box]. My experience at think[box] (both as a user and as an employee) gave me a love and appreciation for making. I currently work for an educational robotics company in DFW and I typically spend my free time mentoring a FRC team (2714 BBQ), going on adventures with friends, or finding great new food.

⚡FRC 2848 The All-Sparks⚡

The All-Sparks was my FRC team in high school. I spent all four years of high school participating in FRC and, looking back, the experience was probably one of the most significant influences in my life as a teenager. Throughout my time on the team, I held the position of programming team captain, design team captain, pitboss, and vice-president. In the 2014 season, we won the World Championship with 254 Cheesy Poofs, 469 Las Guerrillas, and 74 Team CHAOS. My time on the All-Sparks gave me a love for robots and engineering and I'll never forget the memories I made along the journey.
