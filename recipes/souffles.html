Chocolate Soufflés

oooooooh so fluffy

Makes 14 small soufflés (depending on size of ramekins)
Prep time: 2 hours Cook time: 25-30 minutes Cool time: serve immediately
Total: 2 hours 30 minutes

Ingredients

Batter:
  • 2 1/2 cups of milk
  • 9 oz semi-sweet chocolate
  • 1/2 cup sugar

  • 4 1/2 oz sugar
  • 4 1/2 oz bread flour
  • 4 egg yolks
  • 2 whole eggs
  • 2 oz butter
Meringue:
  • 1 1/2 cups egg whites (~4 eggs worth of egg whites)
  • 5oz sugar

Directions

Preheat oven to 400°F.

Combine first three ingredients in a sauce pan. Bring to a boil. Remove from heat.

In a separate bowl, mix sugar, bread flour, yolks, and whole eggs. Add this mixture to first mixture and return to a boil. Remove from heat and add butter. Stir until melted. Set aside to cool.

After the mixture has completely cooled, prepare the meringue. Whip egg whites until fluffy. Add sugar and whip until stiff peaks form. Fold meringue to cooled mixture.

Gather your ramekins. Spray each on the inside with vegetable spray and dust them with granulated sugar. Pour the souffle batter into ramekins.

Place ramekins in baking pan filled with a small amount of water (this is to steam the soufflés while they're baking). Bake for 25-30 minutes and check occasionally to see if water needs refilling.

Remove from oven when soufflés are puffed and have risen above the top of the rims. Serve immediately with whipped cream, chocolate syrup, and fresh fruit (I prefer cherries or raspberries).
