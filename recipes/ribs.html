Baked Ribs

I've been getting a lot of requests from friends and family about my baked ribs recipe. I figured that I might as well put it here so I'm not constantly forwarding people the recipe. This is a easy, low effort recipe that results in tender, fall-off-the-bone pork ribs.

Serves 4-6 people
Prep time: 20 minutes Cook time: 2 hours 30 minutes Rest time: 10-15 minutes
Total: 3 hours 5 minutes

Ingredients

Dry Rub:
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 2 teaspoons black pepper
  • 2 teaspoons paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon celery seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
Other Ingredients:
  • 1 rack of baby back ribs or spare ribs (either works with this recipe)
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce of choice (optional)

Directions

  1. Set oven temperature to 325℉
  2. In a bowl, combine dry rub ingredients and mix until there are no clumps
  3. Rub the spice mix on all sides of the ribs (including the sides) generously
  4. Cover and seal the ribs tightly in aluminum foil. Double wrapping the ribs is optional but recommended in case the first layer leaks.
  5. Place the ribs meat down on the baking pan grab (preferably one with a rim to catch any juices).
  6. Place the ribs on the middle rack of the oven
  7. Bake the ribs for 2 hours and 15 minutes. Rotate the ribs 180 degrees every 45 minutes to ensure even cooking.
  8. Remove the ribs from the oven and raise the oven temperature to 375℉
  9. Open up the foil and turn the over the ribs so they're meat side up
    • Optional: pour BBQ sauce over the ribs if you prefer wet ribs
  10. Bake the ribs uncovered with the meat side up for 15 minutes
  11. Take the ribs out of the oven and let rest for 10-15 minutes
  12. Cut ribs and serve
